Location
Bengaluru, Karnataka

Hush Puppies

Ingredients:

1 cup yellow cornmeal
1/2 cup all-purpose flour
1 tablespoon sugar
1 teaspoon baking powder
1/2 teaspoon baking soda
1/2 teaspoon salt
1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
1/4 teaspoon paprika
1/2 cup buttermilk
1/4 cup finely chopped onion
1/4 cup finely chopped green onion (optional)
1 large egg, lightly beaten
Oil, for frying

Instructions:

In a medium mixing bowl, combine the cornmeal, flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt, pepper, and paprika. Mix well.
In a separate bowl, whisk together the buttermilk, chopped onion, green onion (if using), and the beaten egg.
Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring just until combined. Be careful not to overmix.
Heat oil in a deep fryer or a large pot to 365°F (185°C).
Using a spoon or a small ice cream scoop, carefully drop the batter by tablespoonfuls into the hot oil. Fry until golden brown, turning occasionally, about 2-3 minutes.
Remove hush puppies with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
Serve warm and enjoy!

Dietary Information (per serving - 3 hush puppies):

Calories: Approximately 120 calories
Fat: About 6 grams
Carbohydrates: Roughly 14 grams
Protein: Approximately 2 grams

Dietary Considerations:

Gluten-Free: Use a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend and ensure the cornmeal is labeled gluten-free.
Dairy-Free: Substitute the buttermilk with a non-dairy alternative like almond milk or soy milk. Add a teaspoon of lemon juice to the milk to mimic the tanginess of buttermilk.
Vegan: Along with the dairy-free substitution, replace the egg with a flax egg (1 tablespoon of ground flaxseed mixed with 2.5 tablespoons of water, let it sit for a few minutes until it becomes gel-like).
Low-Sodium: Reduce the salt to 1/4 teaspoon or use a salt substitute.
Low-Sugar: The sugar in the recipe can be reduced to 1 teaspoon or omitted entirely for a savory hush puppy.
