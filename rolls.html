Rosemary Rolls

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup warm milk (about 110째F or 43째C)
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp fresh rosemary, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp melted butter (for brushing)

Instructions:

  1. 1 In a large mixing bowl, combine 1 cup of flour, sugar, yeast, and salt.
  2. 2 Add the warm milk, melted butter, and egg to the flour mixture. Beat until well combined.
  3. 3 Gradually add the remaining flour, about 1/2 cup at a time, mixing until a soft dough forms.
  4. 4 Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes, until smooth and elastic.
  5. 5 Place the dough in a lightly greased bowl, cover, and let it rise in a warm place until it has doubled in size, about 1 hour.
  6. 6 Punch down the dough and divide it into 12 equal pieces. Shape each piece into a ball and place them in a greased baking dish.
  7. 7 Cover the rolls and let them rise again for about 30 minutes, until they have doubled in size.
  8. 8 Preheat your oven to 375째F (190째C).
  9. 9 Brush the tops of the rolls with the melted butter and sprinkle with the chopped rosemary.
  10. 10 Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the rolls are golden brown.
  11. 11 Remove from the oven and let cool slightly before serving.

To fit your dietary needs. Here are some common substitutions:

For Gluten-Free:

  • Use a gluten-free all-purpose flour blend instead of regular flour. Ensure that the blend contains xanthan gum or add it separately (about 1 tsp).

For Dairy-Free:

  • Replace the milk with a non-dairy alternative such as almond milk, soy milk, or oat milk.
  • Use dairy-free butter or margarine instead of regular butter.

For Vegan:

  • Along with the dairy-free substitutions, replace the egg with a flax egg. To make a flax egg, combine 1 tbsp of ground flaxseed with 2.5 tbsp of water and let it sit for a few minutes until it becomes gel-like.

For Low-Sugar:

  • You can reduce the sugar amount or use a sugar substitute like stevia or erythritol. Keep in mind that the texture might slightly change.
